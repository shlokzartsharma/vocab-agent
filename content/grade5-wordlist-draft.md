# Grade 5 Word Lists — DRAFT FOR APPROVAL

## Overview
- Total words: 240
- Total sets: 20
- Words per set: 12
- Sources: SW, WW, FL, CC, T2, SP, CK

### Source Key
- **SW** = Sadlier Vocabulary Workshop Level Orange (Grade 5)
- **WW** = Wordly Wise 3000 Book 5
- **FL** = Flocabulary Word Up Indigo (Grade 5)
- **CC** = Common Core Grade 5 ELA Tier 2
- **T2** = Tier 2 Academic
- **SP** = Singapore/Cambridge Primary Stage 5
- **CK** = CKLA Grade 5

### Selection Criteria
- Every word appears in at least 2 of the above sources
- No overlap with Grade 3 word list (340 words)
- No overlap with Grade 4 word list (240 words)
- No overlap with existing Grade 5 sets 1-48 (576 words)
- All words are harder than Grade 4 level -- more abstract, more academic
- Words are cross-curricular (usable in science, social studies, ELA)
- Words are suitable for narrative storytelling contexts

## 10 Easiest Words
| Word | Justification |
|------|---------------|
| 1. refugee | Frequently encountered in news media; concrete and relatable concept for 5th graders |
| 2. suspense | Students encounter this in reading class regularly when discussing story elements |
| 3. climax | Common in reading instruction from Grade 4 onward for analyzing story structure |
| 4. glossary | Students use glossaries in textbooks regularly from early elementary onward |
| 5. narrative | Common in reading instruction; students write narratives frequently in ELA |
| 6. exile | Concrete concept of being forced to leave; encountered in history and literature |
| 7. commodity | Concrete economic term; frequently encountered in social studies |
| 8. republic | Familiar from Pledge of Allegiance and social studies; concrete political concept |
| 9. ecosystem | Taught in science from Grade 4 onward; a familiar concept for most students |
| 10. advocate | Common in school contexts (advocate for yourself); concrete action word |

## 10 Hardest Words
| Word | Justification |
|------|---------------|
| 1. bureaucracy | Abstract concept about layers of government and organizational structure; multisyllabic |
| 2. metamorphosis | Scientific term with Greek roots; requires understanding of complex biological processes |
| 3. philanthropic | Abstract adjective about charitable giving; uncommon in everyday 5th-grade speech |
| 4. authoritarian | Complex political concept requiring understanding of power structures and governance styles |
| 5. diaspora | Academic term for dispersal of peoples; culturally and historically dense concept |
| 6. symbiotic | Scientific term for mutual biological relationships; abstract and specialized |
| 7. substantiate | Abstract verb meaning to provide evidence; requires sophisticated reasoning about proof |
| 8. ostracize | Social concept of deliberate exclusion; emotionally and conceptually complex |
| 9. insurrection | Political/historical term for armed uprising; requires contextual historical knowledge |
| 10. perpetuate | Abstract verb meaning to cause something to continue indefinitely; conceptually demanding |
